Hi, I'm Piyush!

Professional Career

I am a UI/UX Designer from New Delhi, India. I currently work as a Product Designer at Paytm. I have a year worth of professional experience working in startups to create products from the ground up. I hold a Bachelor degree in Software Engineering from Delhi Technological University, graduating in 2020.

I started off in graphic design during the freshman year of high school. Winning a few competitions and learning about graphic design software like Gimp and Photoshop. I cleared JEE Mains and decided to join DTU, in 2016. Following a few talks with seniors and given my background in graphic design, I started off in UI design in 2017.

I secured my first internship at Prepbytes – a personalised placement training firm. I worked closely with the founders to create designs for the firm’s website. I also did graphic design as well as working with developers to make sure my designs come out pixel perfect.

I worked a UX Designer for RedCarpetUp during the third year of my college. Conducting User Research everyday to redesign a couple of internal apps of the startup. This was the first time I got to work on real wireframes and learn about empathy towards users.

During the summer of 2020, I worked with Onebanc to help create a few modules to secure funding for the firm.

After graduating, I secured an internship at nearbuy. I worked primarily as a UI Designer, converting wireframes to UI Design. I also worked on creating and maintaining a component library, handing off designs, illustrations as well as web design.

As nearbuy got acquired by Paytm, I was converted to a FTE for Paytm in late September, 2020. I have been working on products of both nearbuy and Paytm since.

Rubik's Cube

I first solved a Rubik’s cube in 2011. Learning from YouTube (like I have learned most things in life). Initially, it took me 15 minutes and a piece of paper to solve a 3x3 cube. After almost 10 years of practice and competing, I got it down to less than 10 seconds. I currently enjoy organizing competitions more than solving cubes itself.
